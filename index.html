Hi, this is Yiwen Zhao | 赵艺雯

Welcome to My Portfolio

I'm a second-year MSCV student at the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University, working with Prof. Shinji Watanabe and Prof. László A. Jeni. I'm also collaborating with Prof. Zhongyu Li. I earned my bachelor's degree in CS from Beijing Normal University in June 2024.

My research interests lie in human-centric topics, including multimodal generative models, digital humans, and humanoids.

Goal: I want my research to benefit people—to be useful and fun. I believe multimodal signals have a natural alignment and complementarity with each other. As the inputs and outputs of human beings, they make our lives vibrant and fascinating. At the same time, I want these ideas to be grounded in the same physical world as myself, to interact deeply with people, and to continuously learn from the environment.

Misc: I love figure skating, a beautiful fusion of highly dynamic, precise motion and rich multimodal signals. I also love Winnie the Pooh, the kindest little bear in the world, who has been my best buddy since I was two.

Thanks: I've had the great honor of working with Prof. Teng Han, Prof. Yongzhen Huang, and Prof. Shanghang Zhang during my undergraduate study. There are also many senior colleagues and mentors who have given me invaluable advice and guidance. My deepest thanks go to them, for they generously offered me opportunities to explore and experiment.
